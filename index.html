Research

The high computation demanded by deep learning models motivated the hardware acceleration of Neural Networks. Recent studies have focused on four architectures of accelerators for DNNs, including GPU, FPGA. ASIC, and CGRA.
Because of the high power consumption of GPU, it's not efficient to use them in embedded systems, as in these devices power consumption is a critical challenge. Also, according to the development of Neural Networks, flexibility is the major benefit of FPGAs over the ASIC. Thanks to Deep Neural Network's inherent tolerance against errors, the bit-width of data and operations in Neural Networks can be reduced with a negligible impact on accuracy. In many designed accelerators, they have considered a minimum bit-width that preserves the original accuracy. However, researches have shown that not also the minimum required bit-width varies significantly across variant Neural Networks, but also it differs across different layers of a single Neural Network.

For my master thesis, I have been working on a Multi-Precision Deep Neural Network Accelerator (MpDNN), which is able to change the working bit-width dynamically. The design supports run-time splitting an operator into multiple independent operators with smaller bit-width, effectively increasing throughput when lower precision is required. MpDNN is designed for FPGA, and the multipliers and bit-width adaption mechanisms are optimized for the LUT-based structure of FPGAs. The accelerator's building block is a 16-bit multiply-and-accumulate (MAC) unit made by the 16-bit mpDNN multiplier. The baseline multiplier of this 16-bit MAC unit is an approximate 4-bit multiplier which utilizes 6-input LUTs and the associated carry chain. This approximate multiplier eliminated the carry propagation to reduce the latency, but the input carry is predicted by incorporating some input bits of the previous bit positions in each bit position's calculations. The 16-bit multiplier is made by 16 baseline multipliers, which can be fused recursively to build four 8-bit multipliers or one 16-bit multiplier.
Also, I have written a paper, "MpDNN: Multi-Precision Deep Neural Network Acceleration on FPGA," and we are going to submit it to a known conference or journal as soon as possible.
